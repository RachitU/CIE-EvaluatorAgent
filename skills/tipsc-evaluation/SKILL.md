# TIPSC Evaluation Framework

Five criteria, each scored: **Strong / Weak / Unclear**

## T — Timely
- Is the problem worth solving *now*?
- Long-standing problems are still Timely if: unsolved, increasing, or new technology enables better solutions
- NOT Weak merely because it has existed for years

## I — Important
- Is the pain real, frequent, severe?
- Do people actively want this solved?

## P — Profitable
- Does solving this create meaningful value?
- Do NOT require pricing, revenue projections, TAM, SAM, SOM, CAC, LTV, or business models at this stage
- If commercial viability is unknown, mark Unclear and continue

## S — Solvable
- Can this realistically be solved with available technology and accessible resources?

## C — Contextual
- Does this fit the regulatory, cultural, and socio-economic environment?

## Process Rules
1. First pass: assign Strong / Weak / Unclear to every criterion
2. Skip criteria already marked Strong
3. Investigate Weak and Unclear criteria ONE AT A TIME
4. Ask exactly ONE focused question per turn
5. After each founder response: mark resolved, accept as known risk, or continue probing
6. Move to the next criterion only after the current one is resolved

## Evidence Standards
- A criterion is sufficiently answered with: a reasonable estimate of who is affected, realistic frequency, at least one concrete example, a plausible reason why existing solutions fall short
- Do not demand more precision than a founder can reasonably have
- Do not move the goalposts after receiving a reasonable answer

## Founder Evidence Requirements
A criterion may only be marked Strong when BOTH:
1. Search evidence supports it (if available)
2. The founder has provided: specific customer group, specific pain/problem, frequency, and consequence
